## My Background

My IT career started with **Customer Service** and I continued to do this for 8 years until an opportunity came up for me to begin a new journey working as a developer, which at the time was my passion (still is).

Over those years I worked in industries that required me to become proficient with the likes of Linux, DNS, Nginx, SSLs. I worked for a web hosting company (now GoDaddy) and this is where I did the brunt of my work as a Technical Support Engineer. I would be the first port of call for the customer, both on the phone and via email and assist them with whatever issues they were having at the time, whether that be their server was down, backups were failing, SSLs not renewed or their WordPress site got hacked, to name a few! In this role I was also tasked with helping with development related queries with PHP/MySQL as I was always staying on top of things and continuing to learn it. There were always ups and downs when working with customers in this role as some could be very nice and some could be very angry, but I always put myself in their shoes to try and understand their predicament and help where I could. When a situation was resolved that left the customer happy and left a positive review, it always felt good and warmed the heart knowing I'd made a difference, big or small. It is a great feeling helping those who rely on you the most.

Other roles I have had at **aql** and **VOODOO** also put me in these same situations, with different challenges and different types of customers. My role at **VOODOO**, while still customer-facing, was more on the "DevOps" side of things. Still working on servers like before, but more project based, for the company itself, taking charge of projects assigned to me that in the long term would help customers when using the product. This led to more of an (unofficial) project management role where I would liaise a lot with a development team in Pakistan, helping them understand tasks better and what was needed for the business, as well as some light recruitment.

Even now, in my current role, I help out daily with customer service via Crisp alongside Philo Hermans.

Eventually VOODOO let me move into a development role, but the customer-facing skills I gained have stayed with me throughout.
